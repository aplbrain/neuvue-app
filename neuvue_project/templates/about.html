About Us

We are a team of research scientists and engineers from the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory that developed NeuVue.

NeuVue is a software platform created for large-scale proofreading of machine segmentation and neural circuit reconstruction in high-resolution electron microscopy connectomics datasets. NeuVue provides an intuitive interface for proofreaders to collaboratively view, annotate, and edit segmentation and connectivity data.

NeuVue has everything needed for a high-throughput proofreading workflow: analytical dashboards, data visualization tools, and a simple API that gives stakeholders real-time access to proofreading progress at both individual and global reconstruction levels. NeuVue is intentionally agnostic to the underlying data management backend and can be deployed in an AWS environment.

This research was enabled by the IARPA Machine Intelligence from Cortical Networks (MICrONS) program. Learn more about MICrONS.
